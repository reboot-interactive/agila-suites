Xenon Suite

End-User Guide

v1.0 — March 2026

Getting Started

Welcome to Xenon Suite -- your all-in-one system for managing products, orders, and marketplace connections. This guide will walk you through every part of the system in plain, easy-to-follow steps.

Manage Products

Add, edit, and organize your entire product catalog in one place.

Track Orders

View orders from all your sales channels, update statuses, and see profitability.

Marketplace Sync

Connect to Shopee, Lazada, OpenCart, and TikTok Shop to sync products and orders automatically.

Detailed Reports

See how your business is performing with profit reports, fee breakdowns, and inventory valuations.

Logging In

To start using Xenon Suite, you need an account that an administrator has created for you.

Open your web browser and go to your Xenon Suite URL (the web address your company provided you).
You will see a login page. Enter your username (or email) and password.
Click the Log In button.
You will be taken to the Dashboard -- your home screen.
Forgot your password? Contact your system administrator. They can reset your password from the Users page.

Understanding the Navigation Sidebar

On the left side of the screen, you will see a dark sidebar. This is your main menu. It is organized into collapsible groups. Click on a group name to expand or collapse it.

Here is what you will find in the sidebar:

SectionWhat It Contains
DashboardYour home screen with a snapshot of today's activity and overall stats.
Lazada OrdersQuick link to your Lazada marketplace orders (shows a red badge if there are pending orders).
Shopee OrdersQuick link to your Shopee marketplace orders (shows a red badge if there are pending orders).
OpenCart OrdersQuick links to orders from each connected OpenCart store.
CatalogCategories, Products, Manufacturers, and Options.
SalesOrders and Order Statuses.
Marketplace APISettings and management pages for Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, and OpenCart integrations.
ReportsOrder Profitability, Product Profitability, Marketplace Payouts, Margin by Category, Marketplace Fees, and Inventory Valuation.
SettingsUsers, User Groups, Website Settings, Error Log, and Activity Log.
You might not see all sections. The sidebar only shows sections you have permission to access. If something is missing, ask your administrator to check your user group permissions.

There is also a global search bar in the top right corner. Press the / key on your keyboard or click the search field to quickly find products, orders, or pages anywhere in the system.

At the bottom of the sidebar, you will find the Logout button. Click it to sign out when you are done.

Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It gives you a quick overview of how your business is doing.

Stat Cards

At the very top, you will see a Today's Snapshot bar that shows:

  • Orders Today -- How many orders came in today.
  • Revenue Today -- The total amount earned today.
  • Orders This Week -- How many orders have come in this week so far.

Below that, there are clickable stat cards:

CardWhat It Shows
Total OrdersThe total number of orders across all sources (Direct, Lazada, OpenCart, etc.). Clicking it takes you to the Orders page.
Total RevenueThe total revenue from all orders. It also breaks down revenue by source.
ProductsThe total number of products in your catalog. Click to go to the Products page.
Lazada PendingNumber of Lazada orders waiting to be shipped. Click to go directly to those pending orders.
Shopee PendingNumber of Shopee orders waiting to be shipped.
OpenCart PendingNumber of pending orders from each connected OpenCart store.

Sales Charts

The Sales Chart shows your revenue (as blue bars) and order count (as a green line) over time. You can switch between three views:

  • 30 Days -- Shows daily data for the past 30 days.
  • Monthly -- Shows data grouped by month for the past 12 months.
  • Yearly -- Shows data grouped by year for the past 5 years.

Below the sales chart, you will also see an Orders by Platform doughnut chart. This shows what percentage of your orders come from each platform (Lazada, Shopee, OpenCart stores, Direct). Hover over the chart to see exact numbers.

Sync Status

On the right side of the dashboard, you will see Sync Status panels showing the health of your marketplace connections:

  • A green dot means the last sync happened less than 1 hour ago -- everything is fresh.
  • A yellow dot means the last sync was between 1 and 6 hours ago -- still OK, but getting stale.
  • A red dot means the last sync was over 6 hours ago, or the API token has expired -- needs attention.
  • A gray dot means the connection has never synced.

For each marketplace (Shopee, Lazada, OpenCart), you can see when the last order sync, stock push, and return sync happened, and whether the API token is valid.

The Quick Links section at the bottom left gives you one-click access to Lazada Orders, Shopee Orders, and all Orders.

The Recent Orders table at the bottom right shows your most recent orders with their status, source, and total amount.

Products

The Products page is where you manage everything you sell. Go to Catalog > Products in the sidebar.

The Product List

The product list shows a table with all your products. Each row shows the product's ID, image, name, SKU, stock quantity, price, and status (Enabled or Disabled). You can sort the table by clicking any column header.

Use the Search bar at the top to find products by name, and filter by status (Enabled / Disabled).

Adding a New Product

Click the Add Product button at the top right of the Products page.
Start by typing a Manufacturer name. A dropdown will appear -- select the correct one. (If the manufacturer does not exist yet, you can add one from Catalog > Manufacturers.)
Fill in the General section:
  • Name (required) -- The product name your customers will see.
  • Model (required) -- A model number or identifier.
  • SKU (required) -- Your unique stock-keeping unit code.
  • Quantity -- How many items you have in stock. If you add product options (variants), this is calculated automatically.
  • Status -- Set to Enabled to make the product visible, or Disabled to hide it.
  • Categories -- Search and select one or more categories.
  • Description -- A text description of the product.
Fill in the Pricing & Cost section:
  • Selling Price -- The price you charge customers.
  • Cost -- Your purchase cost (fixed amount).
  • Cost [%] -- An alternative way to enter cost as a percentage of selling price.
  • Additional Cost -- Any extra costs like supplier shipping.
  • The system will automatically calculate the total Product Cost, as well as Profit, Margin %, and Markup %.
Fill in the Shipping & Dimensions section with the product's weight, length, width, and height. This helps with marketplace listings.
If your product comes in different variations (like colors or sizes), add them in the Options section (see Options & Variants below).
Click Save at the bottom of the page.

Editing and Deleting Products

To edit a product, click the Edit button next to it in the product list. You will see the same form as when adding a product, but with the existing details filled in. Make your changes and click Save.

To delete a product, click the Delete button next to it. You will be asked to confirm before the product is removed.

Uploading Images

When adding or editing a product, you will see an Image Manager section. Here is how to use it:

Make sure you have selected a Manufacturer first. The image manager needs this to organize your files.
Click the upload area or drag and drop image files into it.
Your images will appear as thumbnails. You can drag them to reorder -- the first image will be the main product image.
Click the X on any image thumbnail to remove it.

Options and Variants

If your product comes in different variations (like "Red" and "Blue", or "Small" and "Large"), you can add options.

In the Options section of the product form, click Add Option.
Search for an option name (e.g., "Color"). These are pre-defined option types from your Catalog > Options master list.
For each option value (e.g., "Red"), set the quantity, price adjustment (using + or - prefix), and optionally an image.
When you add option values with quantities, the main product quantity is automatically calculated as the sum of all option quantities.
Options also appear in the product list. In the product list, option variants are shown as indented sub-rows under the parent product, so you can see each variant's quantity and price at a glance.

Sales History and Stock History

Each product in the list has two extra buttons:

  • Sales -- Click this to see a history of all orders that included this product. You can see which orders it was sold in, quantities, and totals.
  • Stock -- Click this to see a log of every stock change for this product (e.g., when stock was added, reduced by orders, or manually adjusted).

Bulk Actions

To perform actions on multiple products at once:

Use the checkboxes on the left side of the product list to select the products you want to change. Click the checkbox in the header row to select all products on the current page.
Choose an action from the dropdown: Enable, Disable, or Delete.
Click Apply To Selected.
Confirm the action when prompted.

Orders

Go to Sales > Orders in the sidebar to see all your orders from every source -- manual orders, OpenCart stores, Lazada, and Shopee.

The Order List

The order list shows each order's ID, product image, customer name, source (Manual, Lazada, Shopee, or an OpenCart store name), marketplace order number, status, total amount, and date. You can sort by clicking column headers.

Filtering Orders

At the top of the page, you can:

  • Search by customer name, email, or order ID.
  • Filter by Status using the Status dropdown (e.g., show only "Pending" orders).
  • Filter by Source to see only orders from a specific channel (Manual, Lazada, Shopee, or a specific OpenCart store).

Creating a Manual Order

Click the Add Order button at the top right of the Orders page.
Fill in the customer details -- name, email, and phone number.
Enter the shipping and payment addresses.
Add products to the order by searching for them.
Set the order status and add any notes in the comment field.
Click Save to create the order.

Viewing Order Details

Click the View button on any order to see its full details. The order detail page shows:

  • Meta bar -- Source badge, current status, date, marketplace ID (with a link to the marketplace seller panel), and tracking number (with a link to track the shipment).
  • Customer Details -- Name, email, and phone number.
  • Payment & Shipping Address -- Full addresses and the payment/shipping methods used.
  • Products table -- Each product in the order with its image, name, model, quantity, unit price, total, unit cost, COGS (cost of goods sold), profit, margin %, and markup %.
  • Order Totals -- Sub-total, shipping, and total. If cost data is available, you will also see COGS, shipping cost, any marketplace fees (itemized), and the net profit with margin and markup percentages.
  • Order History -- A timeline showing every status change with timestamps, who made the change, and any comments.

Updating Order Status

On the order detail page, scroll down to the Update Status section.

Select the new status from the Order Status dropdown.
Optionally, type a note in the Comment field.
Click Update Status.

The change will be recorded in the Order History section, along with your name and the timestamp.

Understanding Profitability

Xenon Suite calculates profitability for each order automatically. Here is what the numbers mean:

TermWhat It Means
RevenueThe total amount the customer paid for the products.
COGSCost of Goods Sold -- the total cost you paid for the products (unit cost multiplied by quantity).
Gross ProfitRevenue minus COGS. This is your profit before any fees or shipping costs.
Marketplace FeesFees charged by Shopee or Lazada (commission, service fee, payment fee, etc.).
Shipping CostThe cost of shipping the order.
Net ProfitGross Profit minus marketplace fees and shipping cost. This is your actual profit on the order.
Margin %Net Profit divided by Revenue, shown as a percentage. Higher is better.
Markup %Net Profit divided by total costs, shown as a percentage.
Color-coded profitability. Profit numbers are shown in green when positive and red when negative. Margin percentages are green when 20% or above, yellow-orange when between 10-20%, and red when below 10%.

Categories

Categories help you organize your products into groups. Go to Catalog > Categories in the sidebar.

Creating a Category

Click the Add Category button.
Enter the Category Name.
Optionally select a Parent Category to create a hierarchy (e.g., "Guitars" under "Musical Instruments").
Set the Sort Order to control the display order.
Set the Status to Enabled or Disabled.
Click Save.

Managing Categories

The category list shows each category's ID, name, parent category, sort order, and status. You can:

  • Search for categories by name using the search bar.
  • Edit a category by clicking the Edit button.
  • Delete a category by clicking the Delete button (you will be asked to confirm).
  • Bulk actions -- Select multiple categories using checkboxes, choose "Enable" or "Disable" from the dropdown, and click "Apply To Selected."

Manufacturers

Manufacturers represent the brands behind your products. Go to Catalog > Manufacturers in the sidebar.

Adding a Manufacturer

Click the Add Manufacturer button.
Enter the Manufacturer Name (e.g., "Fender", "Samsung").
Set the Sort Order to control the display position.
Click Save.

Managing Manufacturers

The list shows each manufacturer's ID, name, and sort order. You can search, edit, delete, or bulk-delete manufacturers using the checkboxes and "Delete Selected" button.

Manufacturers are used across the system. When adding a product, you search for a manufacturer. They are also used to organize product images into folders.

Shopee Integration

Xenon Suite connects directly to your Shopee seller account so you can manage orders, returns, and product listings without switching between systems.

Connecting Your Shopee Account

Go to Marketplace API > Shopee > Settings in the sidebar.

On the Settings tab, you will see the Connection section. Enter your Shopee Partner ID, Partner Key, and Shop ID. These are provided to you through the Shopee Open Platform.
Click Save Credentials.
In the Authorization section, click the Authorize button. You will be redirected to Shopee's website to grant access.
After authorizing, you will be redirected back to Xenon Suite. You should see a green "Token valid" message with an expiration date.
Sandbox vs Production. The settings page has a toggle to switch between Sandbox (for testing) and Production (live) mode. Make sure you are using Production mode when working with real orders.

Understanding the Settings Page

The Shopee settings page has three tabs:

  • Settings -- Connection credentials, authorization, environment toggle (sandbox/production), and sync buttons for orders, returns, and stock.
  • API Explorer -- A tool for making direct API calls to Shopee (advanced users).
  • API Logs -- A log of all API requests made to Shopee, useful for troubleshooting.

Syncing Orders and Handling Returns

Go to Shopee Orders in the sidebar (or Marketplace API > Shopee and click Orders).

Fetching Orders

At the top of the Shopee Orders page, you will see a Fetch Orders form. Select a date range (From / To) and optionally a status filter.
Click Fetch Orders. The system will pull orders from Shopee's API and save them locally.
You can also use the Smart Sync option to automatically determine the best date range.

Order Tabs

Orders are organized into tabs by status:

  • All -- Every order.
  • Unpaid -- Orders the buyer has not paid for yet.
  • To Ship -- Paid orders waiting for you to ship.
  • Shipping -- Orders that are on their way to the buyer.
  • Delivered -- Successfully delivered orders.
  • Cancelled -- Orders the buyer or seller cancelled.
  • Failed Delivery -- Orders where delivery failed.
  • Return/Refund/Cancel -- Returns and refund requests.

Handling Returns

Click the Return/Refund/Cancel tab to see all return and refund requests. You can click Fetch Returns to pull the latest return data from Shopee. Each return shows the reason, status, and associated order details.

Shipping and AWB

When viewing an order in "To Ship" status, you can arrange shipping and view the Air Waybill (AWB) information. The system will show you the logistics details, tracking number, and shipping label information from Shopee.

Managing Product Listings

Go to Marketplace API > Shopee > Products to manage your Shopee product listings.

From this page, you can:

  • View products currently listed on Shopee.
  • Push new products from your catalog to Shopee using Profiles.
  • Manage categories and category attributes used by Shopee.

Profiles (under Marketplace API > Shopee > Profiles) are mapping templates that control how your catalog products get translated into Shopee listings, including which category they go into, what attributes they have, and how pricing works.

Logistics (under Marketplace API > Shopee > Logistics) lets you view and manage the available shipping channels for your Shopee shop.

Lazada Integration

Xenon Suite connects to your Lazada seller account for order management, product listings, and fulfillment.

Connecting Your Lazada Account

Go to Marketplace API > Lazada > Settings in the sidebar.

On the Settings tab, enter your Lazada Region, App Key, and App Secret from the Lazada Open Platform.
Click Save Credentials.
In the Authorization section, click Authorize. You will be redirected to Lazada to grant access.
After authorizing, the system will show a green token status with an expiration date.

Like Shopee, the Lazada settings page also has tabs for Settings, API Explorer, and API Logs. There is also an environment toggle for Sandbox/Production.

Syncing Orders and Handling Returns

Go to Lazada Orders in the sidebar.

Fetching Orders

Set a date range using the From and To fields.
Click Fetch Orders to pull orders from Lazada.
You can also use Smart Sync for automatic date range detection.

Order Tabs

Lazada orders are organized into tabs:

  • All -- Every order.
  • Unpaid -- Orders not yet paid.
  • To Ship -- Orders ready to be packed and shipped.
  • Shipping -- Orders in transit.
  • Delivered -- Successfully delivered orders.
  • Cancellation -- Cancelled orders.
  • Failed Delivery -- Orders where delivery failed.
  • Return/Refund/Cancel -- Return and refund requests.

Handling Returns

Click the Return/Refund/Cancel tab. Use the Fetch Returns button to pull the latest return data. You can set a date range or leave it empty to fetch all available returns.

Managing Product Listings

Go to Marketplace API > Lazada > Products to manage your Lazada product listings.

  • View products listed on Lazada with their status, price, and SKU.
  • Use the product form to create or update Lazada listings.
  • Browse Lazada categories and their required attributes under Categories.
  • Set up Profiles to map your catalog products to Lazada categories and attributes.
  • View Lazada Brands that are available for your listings.

Packing and Shipping

For orders in "To Ship" status, Xenon Suite provides packing tools:

  • Packing List -- A printable list of items to pack for selected orders. Great for warehouse staff.
  • Pick List -- A consolidated list of products to pick from inventory across multiple orders.

You can select orders and print these lists to help with your fulfillment process.

OpenCart Integration

Xenon Suite can connect to one or more OpenCart stores to sync products, push stock quantities, and pull orders.

Managing Stores

Go to Marketplace API > OpenCart > Settings in the sidebar.

On the left side, you will see a list of connected stores. Click on a store to view its settings, or click Add New Store to connect a new OpenCart store.

Adding a Store

Click Add New Store from the store list.
Enter the Store Name (a friendly name for your reference).
Enter the Base URL of your OpenCart store.
Enter the API credentials (API URL, username, and key) for the OpenCart store.
Click Save. You can use the Test Connection button to verify it works.

Store Settings

For each store, you can configure:

  • Whether the store is Active or Disabled.
  • API connection details.
  • The store's status in the sync status panel on the dashboard.

Profiles and Syncing

Profiles control which products get synced to which OpenCart store and how they are mapped. Go to the store's profile page from the sidebar (under Marketplace API > OpenCart > [Store Name]).

How Syncing Works

  • Product Sync pushes your catalog products to the OpenCart store, creating or updating products with their names, descriptions, prices, images, categories, and options.
  • Push Stock Quantity sends the current stock levels from Xenon Suite to your OpenCart store, keeping your online store inventory accurate.
  • Order Sync pulls new orders from the OpenCart store into Xenon Suite so you can manage them centrally.
Automatic sync is available. Order sync and stock push can be set up on an automatic schedule (cronjob) so you do not have to do it manually. Ask your administrator to set this up.

OpenCart Orders

Each connected OpenCart store gets its own Orders link in the sidebar. Click it to see orders from that specific store. These orders are also visible in the main Sales > Orders page with an OpenCart badge showing which store they came from.

TikTok Shop

Xenon Suite supports connecting to TikTok Shop. Go to Marketplace API > TikTok Shop > Settings in the sidebar.

Connecting Your Account

Enter your TikTok Shop App Key and App Secret from the TikTok Shop partner center.
Click Save Credentials.
Click Authorize to connect your TikTok Shop account. You will be redirected to TikTok to grant access.
After authorization, you will see the token status and your connected shop details.

Like the other marketplace integrations, the TikTok Shop settings page has tabs for Settings, API Explorer, and API Logs, and a Sandbox/Production environment toggle.

TikTok Shop integration is still growing. Currently, TikTok Shop supports account connection and API access. Additional features like order sync and product push may be added in future updates.

Reports

The Reports section gives you detailed insights into your business performance. Go to Reports in the sidebar.

Order Profitability

This report shows the profitability of every order. You can filter by:

  • Search -- Find specific orders by order number, marketplace ID, or customer name.
  • Date Range -- Use preset ranges (Current Week, Last Month, Last Year, All Time, etc.) or set custom From/To dates.
  • Source -- Filter by Lazada, Shopee, or specific OpenCart stores. Hold Ctrl/Cmd to select multiple sources.
  • Status -- Filter by order status. Hold Ctrl/Cmd to select multiple statuses.

At the top, you will see summary cards showing:

  • Total Orders
  • Total Revenue
  • Total COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)
  • Net Profit

Below the summary, a table lists each order with its revenue, cost, profit, and margin. Click Export CSV to download the data as a spreadsheet file.

Product Profitability

This report shows profitability broken down by product. It has the same filtering options as the Order Profitability report. Summary cards show:

  • Products Sold -- How many different products were sold.
  • Total Units -- Total quantity of items sold.
  • Total Revenue
  • Total COGS
  • Gross Profit

Each row shows a product's name, SKU, total units sold, revenue, COGS, profit, and margin. Click Export CSV to download.

Marketplace Payouts

This report tracks actual payout amounts from Shopee and Lazada. It shows the buyer's payment amount, marketplace fees, seller payouts, and your estimated profit. You can filter by date range and source (Shopee or Lazada), and export to CSV.

Margin by Category

This report shows profitability grouped by product category. For each category, you can see:

  • Units sold
  • Revenue
  • COGS
  • Gross Profit
  • Profit Margin %
  • Profit Markup %

Filter by date range and source to narrow down the results.

Marketplace Fees

This report breaks down all the fees charged by marketplaces (Shopee, Lazada, OpenCart). For each period and platform, it shows:

  • Number of orders
  • Revenue
  • Commission
  • Service Fee
  • Payment Fee
  • Shipping Fee
  • Total Fees

This helps you understand how much each marketplace is costing you in fees.

Inventory Valuation

This report shows the current value of your inventory. For each product, it displays:

  • Product name and SKU
  • Quantity on hand
  • Unit cost
  • Total value (quantity times unit cost)

The total inventory value is shown prominently at the top right. Use the search bar to find specific products by name or SKU.

Exporting data. Most reports have an Export CSV button that downloads the data as a CSV file. You can open this in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet application for further analysis.

Users and Permissions

If you are an administrator, you can manage who has access to the system and what they can do.

Managing Users

Go to Settings > Users in the sidebar.

The user list shows each user's ID, name, username, email, user group, last login time, and action buttons.

Adding a New User

Click the Add User button.
Fill in the user's Name, Username, and Email.
Set a Password and confirm it.
Select a User Group from the dropdown. This determines what the user can access.
Click Save.

To edit a user, click Edit next to their name. To delete a user, click Delete (Administrator accounts are protected and cannot be deleted). You can also click Activity to see that user's activity log.

User Groups

User groups define a set of permissions. Go to Settings > User Groups.

Click Add Group.
Give the group a Name (e.g., "Sales Team", "Warehouse Staff").
In the Permissions section, check the boxes for each permission you want this group to have. You can use Select All or Deselect All for convenience.
Click Save.

Understanding Permissions

Permissions are organized into groups. Each group has a parent permission (which acts as "Select All" for that group) and individual child permissions. Here is what the main permission groups control:

Permission GroupWhat It Controls
Manage CatalogAccess to Products, Categories, Manufacturers, and Options pages.
Manage ProductsAbility to add, edit, and delete products.
Manage CategoriesAbility to add, edit, and delete categories.
Manage ManufacturersAbility to add, edit, and delete manufacturers.
Manage OptionsAbility to manage option types (like Color, Size).
Manage SalesAccess to the Sales section (Orders and Order Statuses).
Manage OrdersAbility to create, view, edit, and delete orders.
Manage Order StatusesAbility to add and edit order statuses.
Manage Marketplace APIAccess to the Marketplace API section (Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop, OpenCart settings).
Manage ShopeeAccess to Shopee settings, categories, profiles, and products.
Manage Shopee OrdersAbility to view and sync Shopee orders.
Manage LazadaAccess to Lazada settings, categories, profiles, and products.
Manage Lazada OrdersAbility to view and sync Lazada orders.
Manage TikTokAccess to TikTok Shop settings.
Manage OpenCartAccess to OpenCart settings and profiles.
Manage OpenCart OrdersAbility to view orders from OpenCart stores.
Manage ReportsAccess to all report pages.
Manage SettingsAccess to the Settings section.
Manage UsersAbility to add, edit, and delete user accounts.
Manage User GroupsAbility to create and modify user groups and their permissions.
Manage Website SettingsAbility to change system settings (company name, logo, timezone, etc.).
Manage Error LogAccess to view system error logs.
Manage Activity LogAccess to view the system activity log.
Permissions are hierarchical. To access a sub-section, the user typically needs both the parent permission and the specific child permission. For example, to view Shopee Orders, a user needs both "Manage Marketplace API" (or "Manage Shopee Orders" specifically) to see the sidebar link.

System Settings

Go to Settings > Website Setting in the sidebar.

General Settings

  • Company Name -- The name displayed in the system and on documents.
  • From Email -- The email address used when the system sends emails.
  • Timezone -- Controls how dates and times are displayed throughout the system. Make sure this matches your local timezone.
  • Activity Log Retention -- How many days of activity log data to keep. Logs older than this will be automatically cleaned up. Default is 90 days.
  • Company Logo -- Upload your company logo to display in the sidebar.
  • Software Logo -- Upload a custom logo for the login page and branding. If not set, the default Xenon Suite logo is used.

Database Maintenance

At the bottom of the settings page, you will find the Database Maintenance section:

  • Purge Marketplace Raw Data -- Clears the raw API response data from Lazada and Shopee orders older than a specified number of days. This frees up database space without affecting your actual order data. Enter a number of days and click Purge Raw Data.
Purging data cannot be undone. Once raw API data is purged, it cannot be recovered. Only use this if you are sure you no longer need the raw data for debugging or reference.

Activity Log

The Activity Log keeps a record of important actions taken in the system. Go to Settings > Activity Log in the sidebar.

What Gets Tracked

The system records events like:

  • Created -- When a product, order, category, user, or other item is created.
  • Updated -- When something is changed (with details of which fields changed and the old/new values).
  • Deleted -- When something is removed.
  • Login / Logout -- When users sign in and out.
  • System events -- Automated actions like order syncs and stock pushes (marked with a "System" badge).

Viewing the Activity Log

The log shows a table with the following columns:

  • When -- The date and time the action happened.
  • User -- Who performed the action (or "System" for automated actions).
  • Action -- The type of action (Created, Updated, Deleted, Login, etc.), color-coded for easy scanning.
  • Subject -- What was affected (e.g., "Product #42 - Guitar Strings"). For updates, you can click to expand and see exactly which fields changed, with old values crossed out and new values highlighted.
  • IP -- The IP address of the user who performed the action.

Filtering the Activity Log

Use the filters at the top to narrow down the log:

  • Search -- Search by subject name.
  • Action -- Filter by action type (Created, Updated, Deleted, Login, etc.).
  • User -- Filter by a specific user.
  • Source -- Show only User Actions or System events.
  • Date Range -- Set From and To dates.

The Clear Old Logs button at the top right removes log entries older than the retention period set in Website Settings.

Tips and Frequently Asked Questions

Common Workflows

How do I sync all my Shopee orders?

Go to Shopee Orders in the sidebar.
At the top, set the From date to the earliest date you want, or leave it empty for the default range.
Click Fetch Orders (or use Smart Sync for automatic detection).
Wait for the system to finish pulling orders. A success or error message will appear.

How do I sync all my Lazada orders?

Go to Lazada Orders in the sidebar.
Set the From and To dates, or leave them for the default range.
Click Fetch Orders.

How do I push stock to all my OpenCart stores?

Go to Marketplace API > OpenCart > Settings.
For each store, find the Push Stock Quantity button and click it.
The system will send the current stock levels from Xenon Suite to that OpenCart store.
Automate with cronjobs. Ask your administrator to set up automatic syncing so orders and stock are kept up to date without any manual work.

What happens when I update an order status?

When you change an order's status:

  • The new status is recorded in the order's history with a timestamp and your name.
  • Stock may be automatically adjusted depending on the status change (e.g., stock is reduced when an order moves to a "shipped" or "completed" status).
  • The change is logged in the Activity Log.

How do I check if my marketplace connections are working?

Look at the Dashboard. The Sync Status section shows colored dots for each marketplace. Green means everything is fine. If you see red or gray dots, go to the marketplace settings page and check your API credentials and token status.

How do I find a specific order from a marketplace?

You have two options:

  • Use the Global Search bar (top right corner, or press /) and type the marketplace order ID.
  • Go to Sales > Orders, type the marketplace order ID in the search field, and click Search.

How do I export my data?

Most report pages have an Export CSV button. Click it to download a spreadsheet of the currently filtered data. You can open the file in Excel, Google Sheets, or any other spreadsheet app.

Best Practices

  • Keep your product costs up to date. Accurate cost data makes your profitability reports meaningful. Use the cost breakdown fields (cost, cost %, additional cost) on each product.
  • Sync orders regularly. If automatic syncing is not set up, try to sync marketplace orders at least once a day to stay on top of pending shipments.
  • Use categories effectively. Organizing products into categories makes them easier to find and improves the Margin by Category report.
  • Review the Dashboard daily. The pending order counts and sync status indicators help you catch issues early.
  • Set up proper user groups. Give each team member only the permissions they need. This keeps things secure and reduces mistakes.
  • Check the Activity Log if something looks wrong. It shows exactly who changed what and when, making it easy to track down issues.
  • Renew API tokens before they expire. Keep an eye on the token expiration dates on the dashboard sync status panel. Re-authorize before they expire to avoid interruptions.
  • Back up before purging. Before using the Purge Raw Data feature in Settings, make sure you have a database backup.
